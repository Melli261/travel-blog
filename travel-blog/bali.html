BALI

Welcome to Bali, Indonesia

Bali, often known as the "Island of the Gods," is a tropical paradise that offers a unique mix of stunning beaches, lush rice terraces, and vibrant culture. From the bustling streets of Kuta to the tranquil temples of Ubud, Bali is full of unforgettable experiences.
Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or a bit of both, Bali has something for everyone. You can start your day surfing in Canggu, explore the sacred Tanah Lot Temple at sunset, or hike up Mount Batur for a breathtaking sunrise view. And don't forget to take some time to unwind on the beautiful beaches, like Seminyak or Nusa Dua.

Top Attractions in Bali

One of the most iconic spots on the island is the Uluwatu Temple, perched high on a cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean. The temple itself is a stunning piece of Balinese architecture, but the real magic happens at sunset. As the sun dips into the ocean, you can enjoy the famous Kecak fire dance, a traditional performance that brings Bali’s rich culture to life in a dramatic setting.
Though Ubud is not known for its beaches, it’s home to some amazing jungle clubs that offer a different kind of retreat. One standout is Cretya Ubud, located amidst the famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces. Cretya combines luxury with nature, offering stunning views of the lush green terraces while you relax in infinity pools or enjoy a meal at their upscale restaurant. It's the perfect escape for those looking to take a break from sightseeing and soak in the beauty of Ubud’s natural surroundings. Whether you’re swimming in the multi-level pools or simply lounging with a drink, Cretya provides a tranquil yet sophisticated experience in the heart of Bali’s countryside.
No trip to Bali is complete without visiting the Tegallalang Rice Terraces. These lush, green terraces are not only visually stunning but also offer insight into the island's agricultural traditions. Whether you decide to hike through the terraces or relax in one of the cafés overlooking the fields, this is a place where nature and culture beautifully intertwine.
If you're in Ubud, make sure to visit the Ubud Monkey Forest, a sanctuary for hundreds of playful long-tailed macaques. The forest is not only a great spot to observe these curious creatures, but it’s also home to ancient temples, making it a unique blend of nature and spirituality.

My Recommendations

If you're planning to explore Bali and its surrounding islands, here are a few tips based on my experience:
For a trip to Nusa Penida, I highly recommend booking a guided day tour instead of trying to explore on your own. While Nusa Penida is breathtaking with its rugged cliffs and turquoise waters, getting around can be tricky. Taxis are very expensive on the island, and most of the key sights aren’t accessible by foot. With a guided tour, you’ll have transportation organized, and you can relax knowing you'll get to see all the highlights without any hassle.
Another island experience I recommend is taking a transfer to Gili Trawangan (Gili T). The island is small and perfect for exploring on foot or by bike, as there are no cars. Gili T is known for its stunning white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters, making it the ideal spot to unwind. Whether you're looking to relax on the beach or snorkel with turtles, Gili T has a laid-back vibe that’s hard to beat.
If you're staying in Bali, renting a scooter is a fantastic way to explore the island. I found it to be the easiest and most affordable way to visit Bali's many waterfalls. Places like Sekumpul, Tegenungan, and Gitgit waterfalls are best reached by scooter, giving you the freedom to go at your own pace and discover hidden gems along the way. Just make sure to ride safely and follow local traffic rules!
